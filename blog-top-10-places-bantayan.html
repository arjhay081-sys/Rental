Top 10 Must-Visit Places in Bantayan Island 2026

Published February 8, 2026 | Travel Guide | 8 min read

Bantayan Island is a tropical paradise in the Visayan Sea, known for its pristine white-sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and laid-back island vibe. Whether you're a beach lover, adventure seeker, or culture enthusiast, this island has something special for everyone. The best way to explore it all? On a motorbike, giving you the freedom to discover hidden gems at your own pace.

Ready to explore Bantayan Island? Here are the top 10 places you absolutely must visit during your stay.

1Virgin Island

Located off the coast of Santa Fe municipality, Virgin Island is one of Bantayan's most sought-after destinations. This uninhabited island is surrounded by crystal-clear turquoise waters, vibrant coral reefs, and pristine white sand beaches that feel completely untouched.

What to do: Swimming, snorkeling, and soaking in the peaceful atmosphere away from the main island crowds. The shallow, calm waters make it great for families with children.

Best time to visit: Early morning (6:00-8:00 AM) for the calmest waters and to enjoy the island before day-trippers arrive.

Pro tip: Bring your own snorkel gear and water shoes. The surrounding waters are teeming with colorful fish and coral formations worth exploring.

2Kota Beach

Kota Beach is one of Bantayan's most unique spots, famous for its stunning sandbar that emerges during low tide. The sandbar stretches out into the sea creating a dramatic walkway surrounded by shallow turquoise water — one of the most photographed scenes on the island.

Why visit: The sandbar is the star attraction, but the beach itself also has calm, shallow waters ideal for swimming and a relaxed atmosphere with nearby eateries and cottages. The area also has historical significance with remains of an old Spanish-era fortress (kota) from which it gets its name.

Activities: Sandbar walking during low tide, swimming, sunset watching, and exploring the Spanish fortress ruins nearby.

Getting there: Easy 10-15 minute ride from Santa Fe town proper. Check tide schedules for the best sandbar experience!

3Sugar Beach

True to its name, Sugar Beach boasts powdery white sand that feels like walking on sugar. This beach offers a more tranquil, less commercialized experience.

Highlights: Incredibly soft white sand, crystal-clear waters, and a peaceful atmosphere. Several small resorts line the beach, offering day-use amenities.

Perfect for: Couples seeking romance, solo travelers wanting quiet time, and photographers looking for that perfect beach shot.

Tip: Visit during weekdays for an even more serene experience with fewer visitors.

4Bantayan Nature Park and Resort

For a change of scenery from beaches, head to Bantayan Nature Park. This eco-resort combines natural beauty with recreational facilities.

What's there:

  • Lagoon-style swimming pools with natural rock formations
  • Cave exploration opportunities
  • Zipline adventure over the lagoon
  • Lush tropical gardens perfect for photos
  • Restaurant serving local and international cuisine

Entrance fee: Minimal fee for day visitors (check current rates upon arrival)

Best for: Families, adventure seekers, and anyone wanting a break from beach activities.

5Ogtong Cave Resort

One of Bantayan's most unique attractions, Ogtong Cave features a natural cave pool filled with cool, fresh spring water - a refreshing contrast to the warm ocean.

The experience: Swim in crystal-clear spring water inside a natural limestone cave. The water temperature stays cool year-round, making it perfect for hot tropical days.

Also at the resort: Beach access, swimming pools, restaurant, and accommodation options if you want to stay overnight.

Fun fact: The cave pool is about 15 meters deep at its deepest point, but there are shallow areas perfect for non-swimmers.

6Madridejos Lighthouse (Faro de Madridejos)

This historic Spanish-era lighthouse stands tall on the northern tip of Bantayan Island in Madridejos town.

Why it's special: Built in the 1800s, this lighthouse offers panoramic views of the surrounding sea and landscape. It's a piece of living history and an excellent spot for photography.

The journey: The ride to Madridejos from Santa Fe takes about 30-40 minutes by motorbike, passing through charming local villages and coastal roads.

Best time: Late afternoon for golden hour photography and stunning sunset views over the ocean.

7Sts. Peter and Paul Parish Church

Located in Bantayan town, this beautiful baroque-style church dates back to 1839 and is one of the oldest churches in the region.

Historical significance: The church has weathered countless typhoons and survived various historical events, making it a testament to the island's resilient heritage.

Architecture: Features stunning coral stone construction, intricate wooden ceilings, and beautiful religious artwork.

Cultural note: The church becomes especially vibrant during Holy Week celebrations and local fiestas.

8Bantayan Public Market

Want to experience authentic local life? The public market in Bantayan town is a sensory adventure showcasing the island's daily rhythms.

What to find:

  • Fresh seafood straight from local fishermen
  • Tropical fruits and vegetables
  • Local delicacies and street food
  • Handmade crafts and souvenirs
  • Traditional Filipino snacks and kakanin (rice cakes)

Best time to visit: Early morning (5-8 AM) when it's most active and fresh seafood arrives from overnight fishing trips.

Must-try: Fresh grilled seafood, danggit (dried fish), and buko (young coconut) juice.

9Hilantagaan Island

For the ultimate island-hopping adventure, take a boat trip to nearby Hilantagaan Island. While you'll need to park your motorbike and take a boat, it's absolutely worth it.

The experience: Uninhabited island with pristine beaches, excellent snorkeling spots, and a true castaway experience. The surrounding waters are teeming with colorful fish and coral reefs.

How to get there: Boat tours can be arranged from Bantayan or Santa Fe (about 30-45 minute boat ride).

What to bring: Snorkeling gear, sun protection, water, and food (no vendors on the island).

10Sunset Beach at Talisay

Last but certainly not least, Talisay offers some of the most spectacular sunset views on the entire island.

Why Talisay: Located on the western side of Santa Fe, Talisay faces west, providing unobstructed views of the sun setting over the Visayan Sea.

The perfect ending: After a day of exploring the island on your motorbike, there's nothing quite like parking by the beach and watching the sky turn brilliant shades of orange, pink, and purple.

Local tip: Several small beach bars and restaurants in Talisay offer front-row sunset viewing with cold drinks and fresh seafood.

Essential Tips for Exploring Bantayan Island by Motorbike

Safety First

  • Always wear your helmet (included free with our rentals)
  • Carry plenty of water and stay hydrated in the tropical heat
  • Apply sunscreen regularly - the island sun is strong!
  • Keep your phone charged for navigation and emergencies

Best Times to Visit

  • Dry season: November to May (best weather)
  • Peak season: December to April (more tourists but best weather)
  • Off-season: June to October (fewer crowds, possible rain, lower prices)

What to Pack for Your Ride

  • Waterproof bag for electronics and valuables
  • Extra clothes (quick-dry fabrics recommended)
  • Reef-safe sunscreen
  • Cash (many places don't accept cards)
  • Snorkeling gear (if you have your own)
  • Camera or smartphone for photos
