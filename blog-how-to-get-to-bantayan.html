How to Get to Bantayan Island from Cebu City: Complete 2026 Travel Guide

February 9, 2026 12 min read Travel Guide

Planning a trip to the stunning white beaches of Bantayan Island? This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about getting from Cebu City to Bantayan Island in 2026, including updated ferry schedules, transportation costs, and insider tips to make your journey smooth and hassle-free.

Quick Overview: Cebu City to Bantayan Island

Total Distance: Approximately 170 kilometers (3-4 hours total travel time)

Route: Cebu City → Hagnaya Port (by bus) → Santa Fe Port (by ferry)

Total Cost: ₱460-570 per person (one way)

Best Time to Travel: Early morning (5:00-7:00 AM) to arrive by midday

Step-by-Step: The Complete Journey

Step 1: Cebu City to Hagnaya Port (North Bus Terminal)

Your journey begins at the Cebu North Bus Terminal located at SM City Cebu in Cebu City. This is where you'll catch a bus heading to Hagnaya Port in the town of San Remigio, northern Cebu.

Bus Route Details

  • Departure Point: Cebu North Bus Terminal (SM City Cebu)
  • Destination: Hagnaya Port, San Remigio, Cebu
  • Travel Time: 3-3.5 hours (depending on traffic)
  • Fare: ₱250-300 per person (air-conditioned bus)
  • Bus Schedule: Buses depart every 30-45 minutes from 4:00 AM to 6:00 PM
  • Bus Companies: Ceres Bus, Sunrays, and other provincial bus operators

Pro Tips for Bus Travel

Book Early Morning: Catch the 5:00 AM or 6:00 AM bus to arrive at Hagnaya Port in time for the morning ferry departures.

Ask the Driver: Tell the conductor you're going to "Hagnaya Port for Bantayan" so they'll remind you when to get off.

Bring Cash: Most buses don't accept cards. Have small bills ready (₱200 or ₱500 notes).

Comfort Items: Bring a light jacket (buses can be cold) and snacks for the journey.

Step 2: Hagnaya Port to Santa Fe Port (Ferry Crossing)

Once you arrive at Hagnaya Port, you'll board a ferry to cross the Tañon Strait to Santa Fe Port on Bantayan Island.

Ferry Route Details

  • Departure Point: Hagnaya Port, San Remigio
  • Arrival Point: Santa Fe Port, Bantayan Island
  • Crossing Time: 60-90 minutes
  • Ferry Fare: ₱200-250 per person
  • Terminal Fee: ₱10-20 per person
  • Ferry Schedule: Multiple trips daily from 5:00 AM to 5:00 PM

Important Ferry Notes

Weather Dependent: Ferry schedules can change based on weather conditions, especially during monsoon season (June-November).

Peak Season: During holidays (Holy Week, Christmas, New Year), ferries get VERY crowded. Arrive 1-2 hours early.

No Advance Booking: Most ferries operate on a first-come, first-served basis. Buy tickets at the port.

Alternative Route: Via Kawit Port

There's an alternative ferry route from Tabuelan's Kawit Port, but it's less frequent and not recommended for first-time visitors.

Alternative: Kawit Port Route

  • Departure: Kawit Port, Tabuelan
  • Arrival: Bantayan Town Proper
  • Schedule: 2-3 trips per day only
  • Cost: Similar to Hagnaya route
  • Recommendation: Stick with Hagnaya Port for your first visit

Step 3: Arriving at Santa Fe Port

Congratulations! You've made it to Bantayan Island. Once you disembark at Santa Fe Port, you have several options to get to your accommodation:

Transportation from Santa Fe Port

  • Tricycle: ₱50-150 depending on distance to your hotel/resort
  • Habal-habal (Motorcycle Taxi): ₱30-100 per person
  • Resort Pickup: Many resorts offer free pickup if you book in advance
  • Scooter Rental: The BEST option for island exploration (more on this below!)

Travel Tips & Insider Advice

1. When to Travel

  • Dry Season (November-May): Best weather, calm seas, consistent ferry schedules
  • Rainy Season (June-October): Possible ferry cancellations, bring raincoat
  • Avoid Peak Holidays: Holy Week and Christmas mean packed ferries and higher prices

2. What to Bring

  • Cash (ATMs are limited on Bantayan Island)
  • Light jacket for air-conditioned bus
  • Snacks and water for the journey
  • Seasickness medicine if you're prone to motion sickness
  • Sunscreen and hat (you'll be outside at the port)

3. Safety Considerations

  • Check weather forecasts before traveling (ferries cancel in bad weather)
  • Keep valuables secure during the journey
  • Stay hydrated, especially during hot weather

4. Money-Saving Tips

  • Travel on weekdays (ferries less crowded, easier to negotiate tricycle fares)
  • Share tricycle rides with other travelers to split costs
  • Bring your own food and drinks instead of buying at ports (marked up prices)
  • Rent a scooter for multi-day stay instead of paying for tricycle rides daily

Returning to Cebu City

The return journey follows the same route in reverse:

  1. Santa Fe Port → Hagnaya Port (ferry)
  2. Hagnaya Port → Cebu North Bus Terminal at SM City Cebu (bus)

Return Trip Advice

Leave Early: If you have an evening flight, take the 5:00 AM or 7:00 AM ferry to be safe.

Buffer Time: Always add 2-3 hours buffer for unexpected delays.

Last Ferry: The last ferry from Santa Fe is usually around 5:00 PM. Don't miss it!

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does the entire journey take from Cebu City to Bantayan Island?

The total travel time is approximately 4.5 to 5.5 hours, including bus travel (3-3.5 hours), ferry crossing (1-1.5 hours), and waiting time at the port.

Q: Can I bring luggage on the bus and ferry?

Yes, both buses and ferries accommodate luggage. Large bags may require an additional fee on some ferries (around ₱20-50).

Q: Is there WiFi on the bus or ferry?

Some air-conditioned buses have WiFi, but it's unreliable. Ferries do not have WiFi. Download offline maps and entertainment before your trip.

Q: Do I need to book ferry tickets in advance?

No, ferry tickets are purchased at the port on a first-come, first-served basis. However, arrive early during peak seasons to secure a spot.

Q: Are there ATMs on Bantayan Island?

Yes, but they're limited and often run out of cash during weekends and holidays. Withdraw money in Cebu City before traveling.

Q: Can I bring a scooter from Cebu to Bantayan Island on the ferry?

Yes, but it's expensive (₱300-500 for the scooter). It's more practical to rent a scooter directly on Bantayan Island from Bobby's Motorbike Rental.
