Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about renting from Bobby's Motorbike Rental

Booking & Rates

How do I book a motorbike?

You can book in two ways:

  • Online: Fill out our booking form which will send your details via WhatsApp
  • Walk-in: Visit our shop directly during operating hours (6:00 AM - 9:00 PM)

Important: We ONLY accept bookings through WhatsApp (+63 907 352 7234). We do not accept phone calls, SMS, or Facebook Messenger.

What are your rental rates?

Our competitive daily rates are:

  • Honda Click: ₱250 per day
  • Honda Beat: ₱250 per day
  • Yamaha Fazzio: ₱350 per day

We also offer discounted rates for weekly and monthly rentals. Contact us via WhatsApp for special rates!

Is there a deposit required?

Payment is required in full upon bike collection. We accept cash payments in Philippine Pesos (₱) only. Deposit requirements may vary - please confirm via WhatsApp when booking.

What's included in the rental?

Every rental includes:

  • Free helmet(s) - up to 2 per bike
  • 24/7 roadside assistance
  • Basic maintenance and support

Requirements

What do I need to rent a motorbike?

To rent from us, you must provide:

  • Age: 18 years or older
  • Valid Government ID: Philippine government-issued ID (National ID, PhilHealth, TIN, Voter's ID, Postal ID) — your ID will be held by us for the entire duration of your rental
  • Accommodation Details: Hotel/resort name and room number
  • Contact Number: Working phone number with WhatsApp

Why do we hold your ID? We keep your government ID while the bike is in your possession. This is for your own safety — in the event of an accident, we can use your ID to properly identify you and assist you immediately. Your ID will be returned to you when you return the bike.

IMPORTANT: We do NOT accept passports or driver's licenses as valid ID. Only Philippine government-issued IDs are accepted.

Do I need a driver's license?

A driver's license is recommended for safety and legal compliance, but we primarily require a valid Philippine government-issued ID — which we will hold for the duration of your rental and return when you bring back the bike. You must have proper riding experience and comply with all Philippine traffic laws while operating the motorbike.

Can foreigners rent from you?

Yes! Foreigners are welcome to rent from us. Please note that we require a valid Philippine government-issued ID which we will hold during your rental period. If you do not have one, please contact us via WhatsApp before booking so we can discuss your options.

Rental Details

What is the minimum rental period?

The minimum rental period is 1 day (24 hours) from your pickup time. We also offer weekly and monthly rental options at discounted rates.

What are your operating hours?

We're open 6:00 AM to 9:00 PM daily, 7 days a week. Bike pickups and returns must be done within these hours unless otherwise arranged.

Can I extend my rental?

Yes! You can extend your rental period by contacting us via WhatsApp before your original return time. Extensions are subject to bike availability and will be charged at the daily rate.

What if I return the bike late?

Late returns will be charged an additional day's rental fee. To avoid extra charges, please return the bike on time or arrange an extension in advance via WhatsApp.

Do I need to refuel before returning?

The motorbike is not provided with a full tank. Fuel is the renter's responsibility for the entire rental period. Please return the bike with at least the same fuel level as when you received it. Running out of fuel during your ride is your responsibility.

Safety & Support

Are helmets provided?

Yes! Helmets are included free with every rental. We provide up to 2 helmets per motorbike. Wearing a helmet is mandatory by law in the Philippines.

What if the bike breaks down?

We offer 24/7 roadside assistance. If you experience any mechanical issues, contact us immediately via WhatsApp at +63 907 352 7234. We will assist you promptly. Repairs due to normal wear and tear are covered by us, but damages due to misuse or accidents are the renter's responsibility.

What happens if I damage the bike?

You are responsible for any damage to the motorbike during your rental period. Repair costs will be assessed and charged to you. In case of accidents, you must:

  • Report the incident immediately via WhatsApp
  • File a police report if it involves third parties
  • Not attempt unauthorized repairs
Is insurance included?

Basic third-party liability insurance may be included (verify at time of booking). However, personal accident insurance is your responsibility. Insurance does not cover reckless driving, DUI, or violations of rental terms.

Location & Area

Where are you located?

We're located in Barangay Talisay, Santa Fe, Bantayan Island, Cebu, Philippines. We're easy to find and conveniently accessible from all major areas of the island. Check our Contact page for the map and directions.

Can I take the bike off Bantayan Island?

No. The motorbike must stay on Bantayan Island at all times. Taking the bike off the island is strictly prohibited and will result in penalties and termination of your rental agreement.

Can you recommend places to visit?

Absolutely! As locals, we know Bantayan Island inside and out. When you pick up your bike, we'll be happy to recommend the best beaches, restaurants, viewpoints, and hidden gems. Some popular spots include:

  • Virgin Island (Paradise Beach)
  • Kota Beach
  • Sugar Beach
  • Bantayan Nature Park
  • Ogtong Cave Resort
  • Madridejos Lighthouse

Policies

What is your cancellation policy?

Cancellations must be made via WhatsApp at least 24 hours before your scheduled pickup time. No-shows will forfeit any deposit or advance payment. We reserve the right to cancel or refuse any reservation.

Can someone else ride my rented bike?

No. Only the registered renter (the person who provided the ID) is authorized to operate the motorbike. Allowing others to ride is a violation of the rental agreement and will void your insurance coverage.

Can I smoke or drink while riding?

Absolutely not. Operating the motorbike while under the influence of alcohol or drugs is strictly prohibited, illegal, and extremely dangerous. Violators will have their rental terminated immediately without refund and may face legal consequences.
